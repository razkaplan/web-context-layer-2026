# CaaS Category Creation: PodCaaSt + CaaSCon + BrightContext

**Owner:** Raz Kaplan | April 2026
**Venue:** Bright Data SF office (events space + recording room)

---

## The Play

Three interlocking programs that feed each other:

1. **PodCaaSt** ("Context Is All You Need") -- weekly podcast from the SF studio. Builds relationships with the 30 people who define this market. Every guest is a future CaaSCon speaker, a future BrightContext contributor, and a future customer.

2. **CaaSCon** -- annual 1-day conference, 75-100 attendees. The podcast guests become the speakers. The content becomes the keynotes. The audience becomes the pipeline.

3. **BrightContext** -- industry blog and research hub. Every podcast episode becomes 2-3 articles. Every CaaSCon talk becomes a deep-dive. Compounds SEO over 12+ months into the authoritative source for "context as a service."

The flywheel: Podcast builds relationships and content. Content feeds the blog and SEO. Blog builds audience. Audience fills the conference. Conference produces more content. Repeat.

## Part 3: BrightContext -- The CaaS Industry Blog

BrightContext is not a Bright Data blog. It is the industry publication for Context as a Service. Bright Data is the publisher, not the subject. Think of it like how Salesforce publishes content about CRM strategy, not just Salesforce features. The brand earns authority by serving the category.

brightcontext.com (or brightdata.com/context as a subdomain to inherit domain authority)

**Recommendation:** Start as brightdata.com/context to leverage existing DA. Spin out to brightcontext.com once traffic justifies it (10K+ monthly visits).

5. Executive positioning (Raz as the voice of the category)

**1. Inkhouse** (recommended)

- Why: Deepest data infrastructure client portfolio. Named AOR for 6 AI companies in Q1 2025. They know how to position data infra companies. 140+ people, 7 cities.
- Retainer: $15,000-$30,000/month
- Best for: Full category creation. They've done this for Databricks (data lakehouse) and can replicate for CaaS.

**2. RH Strategic**
- Clients: Technology companies across cybersecurity, AI, data/analytics
- Why: One of the few firms that explicitly markets "category creation initiatives" as a core service. Strong analyst relations (critical for Gartner/Forrester recognition).
- Retainer: $10,000-$25,000/month
- Best for: Analyst relations + category definition. Get CaaS into the analyst vocabulary.

**3. Firebrand Communications**
- Clients: B2B tech across fintech, cybersecurity, AI, data, cloud
- Why: SF-based (local to Bright Data office). Integrated PR + growth marketing + content + ABM. Boutique, hands-on. Right size for a category creation project.
- Retainer: $7,500-$15,000/month
- Best for: Tightest integration with the three programs. They can support podcast promotion, conference PR, and BrightContext content distribution as one program.

### Alternative Model: Fractional Head of Comms + Freelance

Instead of a traditional PR firm, hire a fractional VP of Comms (ex-agency, 2-3 days/week) who owns the narrative and coordinates:
- A freelance podcast producer
- A freelance event coordinator
- A freelance writer pool for BrightContext
- Direct media relationships

Cost: $8,000-$12,000/month for the fractional lead + freelance costs.
Pro: More control, deeper integration, single owner.
Con: No agency bench strength. If the person leaves, the program stalls.

## Revenue Model: How This Turns Into Pipeline

### Direct Revenue Paths

**Path 1: Podcast guests become customers**
- 20 guests in Year 1. At least 12 run companies that actively scrape the web.
- If 3 of them convert to Bright Data customers within 12 months: conservative ACV $50K-$200K each.
- **Expected: $150K-$600K in direct pipeline from podcast relationships.**

**Path 2: CaaSCon attendees enter sales funnel**
- 75-100 attendees, hand-selected (ICP fit A or B from the universe).
- Industry benchmark: 15-25% of niche event attendees take a meeting within 60 days.
- That's 12-25 discovery calls from one event.
- At 20% close rate with $75K average ACV: 2-5 deals.
- **Expected: $150K-$375K in pipeline from CaaSCon.**

**Path 3: BrightContext organic inbound**
- Timeline: 9-15 months to meaningful organic traffic (10K+ monthly visits).
- B2B conversion rate from organic: 2-3% to lead, 10-15% lead-to-opportunity.
- At 10K monthly visits: 200-300 leads/month, 20-45 opportunities/month by Month 18.
- B2B organic search generates 44.6% of all revenue on average.
- **Expected (Month 12-24): $200K-$500K annual pipeline from organic.**

**Path 4: CaaSCon sponsorship offsets costs**
- Niche B2B event with curated data/AI audience.
- Title sponsor: $15,000-$25,000. Gold sponsors (2-3): $5,000-$10,000 each. Session sponsors (2-4): $2,500-$5,000 each.
- **Expected: $30,000-$60,000 in sponsorship revenue (offsets conference costs entirely).**

**Path 5: Category authority multiplies all other GTM**
- When Gartner/Forrester recognizes "CaaS" and Bright Data is the reference vendor, every enterprise RFP conversation shifts.
- The 120-account campaign (from the DaaS master brief) converts at higher rates because prospects already know the brand from the podcast/blog/conference.
- This is the hardest to quantify but the highest-leverage outcome.

- **BrightContext:** Organic traffic compounds. Meaningful inbound starts Month 9-12. Revenue from organic leads starts Month 12-18.
- **Full program breakeven:** Month 10-14 in the lean scenario.

## Decision: What to Do First

If budget forces sequencing rather than launching all three simultaneously:

1. **Start the podcast first (Month 1).** Lowest cost, highest relationship ROI. Every guest is a future conference speaker and blog contributor. You cannot build the conference or the blog without the network the podcast creates.

2. **Launch BrightContext at Month 2.** The podcast generates content immediately. Repurpose transcripts into articles. Start building SEO equity early because it compounds.

3. **Announce CaaSCon at Month 3.** By then you have 10 episodes, a growing blog, and relationships with 10+ industry leaders. The conference has gravity.

The podcast is the engine. Everything else is distribution.
