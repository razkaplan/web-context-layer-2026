The Core Shift

DaaS is no longer about
delivering raw data

LLMs have changed what a data product is. A new generation of companies uses AI to synthesize information across dozens of sources, extract non-obvious relationships, and deliver structured intelligence rather than raw tables. Enigma maps hidden ownership and business relationships from fragmented public records. Revelio Labs builds a real-time workforce intelligence graph from public signals. YipitData[1] turns web-scraped transaction signals into institutional-grade market intelligence. RavenPack[2] runs NLP across millions of news articles per day for financial sentiment signals. Clay[3] aggregates 100+ data sources in real time to power AI-driven GTM workflows. Exa[4] re-indexes the web semantically so AI agents can search by meaning, not keyword. These are not traditional DaaS companies with better packaging — they are a structurally different product category.

Traditional DaaS

Raw data delivery

  • ·Single-source vertical datasets
  • ·Batch exports, CSV pipelines, quarterly refresh
  • ·Scraping one or two target sites
  • ·Data sold as-is, no enrichment layer
  • ·Compliance: a contractual checkbox
  • ·Moderate scraping scale
AI-Native DaaS

Structured intelligence

  • →Multi-source aggregation across dozens of sites and data types
  • →LLM enrichment pipelines running on live, real-time retrieval
  • →Relationship extraction, entity resolution, semantic search
  • →LLMs hallucinate on stale data — live grounding is non-negotiable
  • →SOC 2 Type II + ISO 27001 required to close enterprise contracts
  • →10–100x higher scraping scale than traditional DaaS

The Infrastructure Requirement

Why web access infrastructure is
the critical dependency of this market

Every AI-native DaaS company in this market depends on continuous, high-volume access to the public web. The data feeding their LLM enrichment pipelines, their entity resolution graphs, their real-time financial signals, comes from web pages that actively resist automated access. LinkedIn, Zillow, major financial sites, and news outlets deploy IP blocking, browser fingerprinting, and CAPTCHA middleware that defeats standard residential proxies within days of scaling at production volume. These are not inconveniences — they are the primary technical barrier to building a reliable data product.

The infrastructure required to solve this is not a scraper. It is a network layer: 72 million residential IPs with clean, independent behavioral profiles, request routing that mirrors organic user patterns, session management that survives bot detection, and a headless browser environment that renders JavaScript as a real human browser would. Building this in-house takes years and produces a fraction of the reliability of purpose-built infrastructure. Research from Monte Carlo Data shows 40% of engineering time goes to maintaining broken data feeds[8] — the maintenance loop is structural, not a skill gap.

The second dependency is freshness for LLM accuracy. AI-native DaaS companies feed live web data directly into LLM enrichment pipelines. Research on RAG hallucination identifies three production failure modes[9]: fabricated statistics (the model generates plausible-sounding numbers from retrieved text), incomplete retrieval (vector search misses relevant data scattered across sources), and out-of-domain fabrication (when no relevant live data is retrieved, the model invents an answer). Live web grounding is the only structural fix.

The third dependency is compliance. SOC 2 Type II is now standard in US enterprise vendor reviews[10] and ISO 27001 is increasingly mandatory in EU procurement. The compliance stack of the infrastructure layer passes through to the data product. A company cannot retrofit certifications onto an in-house scraping stack — the compliance story has to come from the vendor.

Why building this in-house fails
Reason 01
Shared IP pools degrade at scale
Standard residential proxy pools accumulate blocks across all users simultaneously. By the time a team scales to production volume, the pool's behavioral reputation with target sites is already compromised. Detection is triggered by traffic pattern analysis, not individual request volume.
Reason 02
Sites change faster than teams can ship
Every DOM update, new bot middleware deployment, or JS rendering change breaks the scraper at runtime regardless of how well it was written. AI coding tools make writing the initial scraper trivial. They do nothing for the runtime when the target site changes. The maintenance loop is structural.
Reason 03
Compliance cannot be retrofitted
SOC 2 Type II and ISO 27001 require 12 months of documented operational controls, audited by an independent third party. An enterprise security review arrives before the certification is ready. The compliance documentation has to come from the infrastructure vendor from day one.
